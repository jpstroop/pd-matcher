## Caveats — why a null result here is not a complete answer

The diagnostic measures the 246 effect on **labeled MATCH vault entries only**. The vault has a built-in selection bias against the exact population this change is designed to help:

- Every vault MATCH entry is a MARC the matcher already paired with a CCE entry well enough to surface as a top-1 candidate and pass the floor under the old (245-only) code. Those records, by construction, already have a strong 245-vs-CCE signal — they are the cases least likely to flip from a retrieval/scoring widening.
- The MARCs the 246 change is designed to help — records lost because the 245 string differs meaningfully from how CCE catalogers transcribed the title (e.g. CCE has the cover title; MARC has the title-page title) — never reach the labeler as a MATCH verdict because they never made it through retrieval+floor. They are invisible to this diagnostic.

The corpus-level audit (`marc_246_prevalence_2026-06-08.md`) showed ~2,500 records carrying a CCE-likely 246. Whether any of those are MARCs whose 245 is too noisy to match alone is exactly what this diagnostic cannot measure on the current vault.
